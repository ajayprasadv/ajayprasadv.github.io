AI in Security and Security in AI

Cyber Security guys… Say Cheers!! AI systems are becoming increasingly complicated, so protecting them needs a multifaceted strategy.

By Dr. Ajay Prasad, 23rd October 24, 2024

We all know, that AI is here to stay… and your job equally gonna be on fire… new avenues are smearing in… Let us view it in a macro way. That is, new cyber security avenues can be seen in two parts A) AI in Security and, B) Security in AI. The AI in Security means we have to look into the areas where we can utilize AI to solve/Enhance the conventional security approaches. AI has been used to detect malware, spam, etc. But, now, more AI can be brought in and into very diverse areas of cyber security. The other phrase Security in AI is showing up and is what I feel, is more challenging, more promising, and, more disruptive in the field of Cyber Security.

Let’s visit briefly both these aspects…

AI in Security

AI is and will be helping cyber security in several ways. AI-driven systems can identify malware, phishing attempts, and other threats more rapidly than conventional methods by analyzing data and recognizing anomalies in patterns. Artificial intelligence facilitates automatic responses to minor security problems, diminishing the necessity for human involvement. AI assists in anticipating potential threats by utilizing machine learning algorithms to predict where vulnerabilities may be exploited. By evaluating biometric data (such as facial recognition) or behavior-based analytics, artificial intelligence (AI) enhances authentication systems and ensures more secure access control. But, hey!! Don’t be fooled!! Hackers are also smart! In the digital security landscape, a dynamic arms race is emerging as cybercriminals increasingly employ AI to develop advanced attack strategies, presenting challenges for AI in cyber security.

Security in AI

By the phrase "security in AI" I mean, the procedures and policies designed to shield artificial intelligence (AI) systems against malevolent intrusions, manipulation, and weaknesses that can jeopardize their availability, integrity, and secrecy. Ensuring the security of AI is crucial as it gets more incorporated into crucial systems like healthcare, finance, and defense. AI models can be controlled by malicious actors by providing them with false or altered data (such as adversarial inputs) to cause the system to make inaccurate judgments or predictions. Anything from fraud detection algorithms to picture recognition systems may be impacted by this. Attackers may tamper with the training data that AI systems utilize, adding minute inaccuracies (Data Poisoning) that lead to unexpected or biased model behavior. Attackers may be able to recreate or alter the system for nefarious reasons by stealing or reverse-engineering AI models, which may reveal confidential information. Large volumes of personal data are frequently used by AI systems. Security and data privacy are vital, particularly when using AI to handle sensitive information for decision-making.

AI systems are becoming increasingly complicated, so protecting them needs a multifaceted strategy that includes strong encryption, frequent monitoring, secure development techniques, and methods to identify and thwart potential attacks on AI models.
